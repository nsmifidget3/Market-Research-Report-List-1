<p><p>The thermal conductivity measurement device market can be categorized into two key types based on temperature ranges: room temperature range and high temperature range. Room temperature range devices are designed to measure thermal conductivity in materials at standard laboratory conditions, typically between 20 to 30 degrees Celsius, suitable for polymers and some metals. High temperature range devices, on the other hand, are engineered to assess materials' thermal properties at elevated temperatures, often exceeding 1000 degrees Celsius, essential for applications in aerospace, automotive, and energy sectors.</p></p>
